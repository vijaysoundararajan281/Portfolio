IEEE · RAEEUCCI 2024 · DOI 10.1109/RAEEUCCI61380.2024.10547857

Streamlining Deep-Learning-Based Alzheimer's Classification: A Simplified Approach with ADASYN

V. Saravanan, T. Sidharth, S. Vijay, S. Sankar Ganesh — KPR Institute of Engineering & Technology

Diagnosing Alzheimer's from brain MRI is hard partly because the data is badly imbalanced — some dementia stages have thousands of scans, others only a handful. Heavy models tend to overfit and quietly ignore the rare classes.

Our approach keeps the model deliberately small and fixes the imbalance at the data level instead. We pair a compact 2D CNN (two conv blocks, dropout, three dense layers) with ADASYN, which synthesises new samples for the under-represented dementia stages so the network actually learns them. The moderate-dementia class, for instance, went from 45 samples up to roughly 1,900 before training.

On a four-class OASIS-derived MRI dataset the model reached 98% accuracy with balanced precision and recall across every severity level — matching or beating heavier architectures at a fraction of the complexity.
